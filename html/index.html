Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley - Frankenstein

Welcome to the mini digital edition of Mary Shelley's Frankenstein. Made as a project for Text as Data II, taught at the University of Antwerp.


After a visit to Lord Byron's Swiss Villa Diodati in the gloomy summer of 1816, Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley started to write her book Frankenstein, or the Modern Prometheus (1816). Shelley herself came from a family of writers and prolonged this tradition by writing this gothic-romantic novel. It tackled questions concerning technolocial advancement, spirituality and interhuman relations. This present rendition aims to give way to an academic edition of Frankenstein by uncovering the transcriptions of the original manuscript by her hand and that of her husband Percy Bysshe Shelley. He edited the original manuscript and gave his own remarks.
